The Recipe

The Essentiel Purée

Chef Christophe's recipe — serves 4 · 35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Ratte potatoes (or Yukon Gold)
  • 250 g cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 200 ml whole milk, warm
  • Fine sea salt, to taste
  • White pepper, freshly ground
  • Fleur de sel, to finish

Method

1

Cook the potatoes whole and unpeeled in well-salted cold water. Bring slowly to a boil; simmer 25 min until a knife passes through without resistance.

2

Drain and peel immediately while still steaming. Pass through the Rösle ricer on the fine disc directly into a saucepan over low heat.

3

Work in the cold butter cube by cube with a wooden spoon — never a whisk. Let each piece absorb before adding the next.

4

Stream in warm milk gradually, stirring continuously, until the purée reaches a silky, ribbon-dropping consistency.

5

Season generously with fine salt and white pepper. Serve immediately with a pinch of fleur de sel.

★

Chef's note — The secret is the ratio: never less than 1 part butter to 4 parts potato by weight. Cold butter worked in slowly over heat creates an emulsion no warm butter can replicate. Do not over-work once the milk is in — you want silk, not glue.

About the Rösle Ricer+

The Rösle potato ricer is the standard in professional kitchens across Europe. Formed from a single piece of 18/10 stainless steel, built for decades of daily service — completely dishwasher safe and food-safe certified.

  • 18/10 stainless steel — food-safe, dishwasher safe
  • 3 interchangeable discs: fine (purée), medium (gnocchi), coarse (vegetable press)

Our Story

Born from a
dinner table

One man's obsession with starred kitchens — and the idea that a great meal shouldn't end when you leave the restaurant.

The Founder

Victor Poisson,
hospitality at heart

Victor Poisson grew up between France and the United States, spending his formative years at tables that mattered — restaurants where every plate told a story, where the chef's name was spoken with the same reverence as a painter or a couturier.

Settling in New York City, he became a regular at the city's finest starred restaurants. Each dinner was an experience he wanted to hold onto — the weight of a particular knife, the geometry of a handmade ceramic, the fragrance of a sauce he could never quite replicate at home.

He realised he was not alone. Every great restaurant has guests who ask "where can I buy this?" — and too often, the answer is nowhere. The souvenir of a remarkable meal existed only in memory.

Victor founded Plated — a platform that gives top restaurants a shoppable boutique, and gives their guests a way to bring a piece of that experience home. From their kitchen to yours.

Victor Poisson
Founder & CEO — Plated, New York
